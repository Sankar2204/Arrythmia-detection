What is Cardiac Arrhythmia?

An irregular heartbeat caused by abnormal electrical activity in the heart. The heart may beat too fast, too slow, or irregularly, affecting the blood flow to the body.

Tachycardia

A resting heart rate that is greater than 100 beats per minute. Warning signs include rapid pulse and chest flutter.

Bradycardia

A resting heart rate of less than 60 beats per minute. Can lead to inadequate blood flow, causing fatigue and fainting.

Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)

Irregular and rapid heart rhythm originating in the atria. Increases stroke risk massively if left untreated.

Ventricular Fibrillation

A critical, life-threatening condition where the ventricles quiver uselessly instead of pumping blood. Requires instant shock.

Premature Heartbeats

Extra, early beats that briefly interrupt your regular heart rhythm. Often felt as a "skipped" beat in the chest.

Medical Treatments

  • Anti-arrhythmic drugs: Restore normal heart rhythm.
  • Beta-blockers: Slow down the heart rate and calm irregular beats.
  • Blood thinners: Prescribed for AFib patients to dramatically reduce stroke risk.

Advanced Procedures

  • Catheter Ablation: Destroys small areas of heart tissue causing abnormal signals.
  • Pacemaker: Implanted device that regulates slow heartbeats.
  • Defibrillator (ICD): Monitors rhythm and delivers life-saving shocks for V-Fib.

Lifestyle & Wellness Guide

Managing arrhythmia goes beyond the hospital. Daily habits are critical for a healthy heart rhythm.

Heart-Healthy Diet

  • • Low salt & low saturated fat
  • • Increase Potassium & Magnesium
  • • Superfoods: Bananas, spinach, salmon

Daily Habits

  • • Regular moderate exercise
  • • Strict avoidance of smoking/alcohol
  • • Reduce or eliminate caffeine

Monitoring

  • • Daily pulse checks
  • • Smartwatch ECG monitoring
  • • Stress management (Yoga, Meditation)
